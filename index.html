SECTION 1.6 — THE WHY-NOW QUESTION

Why this hasn't been done — the 18–30 month adoption window

The most uncomfortable diligence question for any thesis like this one: if it's as obvious as the deck makes it sound — fragmented market, founder succession, AI compression, dual-side AVEVA pedigree — why hasn't Sverica, Tailwind, CenterOak, or Kohlberg already executed it in Houston? The honest answer is a three-step adoption ladder, and most named platform competitors are still on Step 1.

STEP 1 · WHERE THEY ARE

Financial integration (18–24 months)

Chart-of-accounts harmonization, ERP consolidation, back-office centralization, sales-comp normalization, customer-contract migration. Necessary; not paradigm-shifting. The named platforms in Section 1.1 are mostly still here. E Tech Group's five bolt-ons each mid-integration; ENTRUST exited Kohlberg before Step 2; GrayMatter under Tailwind has had ~24 months and one add-on so far.

STEP 2 · LEADING EDGE

Operational standardization (18–24 months)

Shared engineering libraries, standardized methodology gates, consolidated tooling, recurring-services expansion. InflexionPoint's Process Innovations acquisition in October 2025 looks like a Step-2 move targeting the AVEVA PI APM capability. This is where most of the platform-multiple expansion gets earned.

STEP 3 · THIS PLAN

AI-augmented delivery + outcome-based pricing

Almost no one is here yet at scale. Step 3 requires breaking the billable-hour incentive structure that Steps 1 and 2 still operate inside — a paradigm shift, not a process improvement. Cowork SDA was built during the operator's Schneider tenure as a working Step-3 substrate, before any named platform has committed to it.

The window. The adoption gap between leading-edge thinking inside the AVEVA partner program and current SI execution is currently estimated at 18–30 months. By acquiring a smaller GCAP-archetype SI where Step 1 closes faster (single founder, no integration debt) and starting Step 3 on Day 1 with tooling that already exists (Cowork SDA + UCWH discipline), the plan is structurally positioned to be 18–24 months ahead of the platforms still working through Steps 1 and 2 at exit time. Section 5 (Transformation Plan) and Section 5.3 (Pricing-Model Transformation) are built around capturing that window.

Caveat: the "18–30 months ahead" framing is the operator's direct read of the market based on his Schneider seat (2 yrs 8 mos at AVEVA's parent company, ending January 2026) and ongoing relationships with multiple named platform leadership teams. 5.3 Pricing Model Transformation — the load-bearing piece of the thesis

The compression thesis (engineering hours reduced 30–50% in Year 1 → 50–70% by Year 3) does not flow directly to EBITDA in a T&M or hours-priced fixed-fee shop, because the labor savings accrue to the customer. The honest framing of how the bridge is realized — and the load-bearing piece of this whole plan — is a parallel pricing-model transformation running alongside the AI factory rollout.

TIER 1 · YEAR 1

Risk-share fixed-fee on existing project work

Convert T&M and hours-based fixed-fee bids to risk-share fixed-fee: customer pays a fixed price built on legacy-hour estimates with a 10–20% trim; SI carries productivity risk; if AI-augmented delivery comes in under hours, SI keeps the delta. Most defensible flow-through for the Year-1 piece of the EBITDA bridge — labor savings DO flow to EBITDA in this contract structure.

TIER 2 · YEAR 1–2

Outcome-based managed services

MSA layer (~35% of GCAP's mix at acquisition) is the highest-leverage place to push outcome-based pricing. Shift KPIs from "X hours per month" to outcome metrics — uptime SLA, MTTR, alarm rationalization coverage. AI-driven synthesis absorbs labor; customer pays for outcome. Gross margin lift: 20–30 points vs. T&M.

TIER 3 · YEAR 2–3

Productized offerings

Generalize the ETAP Discovery Skill pattern: System Platform Quick-Start, Historian/PI Standup, Pharma MES Validation Pack, Water/WW Compliance Bundle. Sell as fixed-fee productized engagements at SaaS-like gross margins (~70–80%). Year 4 productized layer target: $4–6M annualized. Also licensable to other AVEVA SIs via the partner program — upside, not core.

The competitive bid-down counter. Won't competitors match the AI cost savings and bid us down? The bid-down spiral happens in T&M and hours-based fixed-fee — the tiers this transformation moves away from. It does not happen in outcome-based or productized pricing because the comparison frame is different (customer is buying outcomes, not hours). The pricing transformation IS the defense against bid-down. The platform ahead on Tier 2 + Tier 3 captures share at the same margin while platforms still on Tier 1 race each other to the bottom on hours.

Year-1 PRIMARY KPI — pilot-project output-per-dollar measurement (the bridge from thesis to evidence): at least one live AVEVA System Platform project delivered with scope-delivered measured against the baseline FDS estimate as the primary metric, plus engineering hours as a secondary tracking metric. Expressed as: "FDS baseline scope X delivered for $Y of project labor; actual scope X+Δ delivered for the same $Y." The Δ is the additional scope captured per project-dollar — the metric that flows to EBITDA under risk-share fixed-fee (Tier 1) and that opens the next outcome-based-MSA conversation under Tier 2. Target: 30–50% additional scope captured on factory-aligned System Platform / SCADA infrastructure work; 20–35% on MES execution-logic work (more conservative because customer-specific business logic compresses less than templated infrastructure).
